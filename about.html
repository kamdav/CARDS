About CARDS

The ethos of the club is based on the acronym CARDS.  Whether or not members bring guests the seating arrangements, apart from the Chairman and the Speaker, are settled on arrival by the drawing of playing cards.

The procedures for the evening are then settled by the Chairman inviting the Speaker to draw three further playing cards:  the holder of the first being asked to say Grace; of the second to propose the Loyal Toast; and of the third to propose the vote of thanks to the Speaker.

Chairmen are elected for a one year term.  The other officers are the Hon Secretary and the Hon Treasurer whose appointments are not time-limited but are renewed annually.  Additionally, the committee comprises the intending chairman for the following year, together with two other members, usually the two immediate past chairmen.

The annual subscription is £30.00.  The costs of the dinners are shared afterwards between the members who were present, on a “mess bill” basis. In allocating places at the dinners, priority is given to members themselves but, subject to that, members may bring as many guests as they wish if there are places available.

The Chairman for the year invites the speakers who are encouraged to speak for approximately 30 minutes with ten minutes maximum for questions.  The subjects addressed by guest speakers cover a wide variety of interests and are not necessarily related to the financial or accounting world.

Past speakers have included senior politicians, civil servants, business people and individuals in positions of leadership of all kinds, including figures from the media.

Members are welcome at every meeting.  Guests do not have to be Chartered Accountants and may include spouses or family members if wished.  Potential members should feel able to commit themselves in principle to attend at least two dinners in each year.
